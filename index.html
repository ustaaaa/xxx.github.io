Technology at Zeustone

The physical world and the financial world are both highly non-linear and dynamic but we believe that we can understand them. Instead of using the conventional model-driven approaches which rely on probably unrealistic assumptions to design complex models, we turn to data-driven methods that understand the model directly from real data.

We exploit the most recent advances in machine learning and high-performance computing to model, analyse, and predict the complex patterns, dependencies and relations between different assets, entities and even people.

Our engineers have built modern cognitive systems that perform data aggregation from enormous sources including but not limited to the real-time financial market, global political and economic events, as well as the social medias. Our researchers have developed different deep neural networks for market indicator extraction, black-swan event detection, market sentiment judgment, which are used by our economists and trading teams for trading-decision making and risk control.

We have also established 24*7 smart trading systems for different assets (commodity, forex, stock, cryptocurriences) in various markets across US, Asia and Europe.

Innovation at Zeustone

We are a highly innovative team. Our core team members are all Ph.D and had years of research experience in top-tier universities and financial institutes in Europe. We conduct serious academic research and publish our results regularly.

We are not satisfied by only using adopt exsisting machine learning methods as a tool (try and test as many others), instead, we invent new machine learning based tools specifically for asset management. This is achieved by defining new optimization criterion, regularization functions for the neural networks, and by intergating theories in economics into such design. This is helpful, for instance, to risk management since we are able to design a system that meets the risk tolerance for each individual investor.

The patterns in the real world change rapidly and our system is able to learn them in a self-contained mannner. The evolution of the market automatically provides new labels that are prerequisite for supervised model updating; fast and stable algorithms are adopted to retrain the model parameters. We utilize transfer learning to slove the low-resource data problem in many assets, and also use semi-supervised learning such as teacher-student models to save the high cost of data tagging in many problems.

Although large models are able to represent more complex patterns, we have developed knowledge distilling methods to minimize the model, in order to achieve good generalization and fast inference in practial scenarios.
